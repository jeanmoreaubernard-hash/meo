Legal & Financial Structure for MEO

⏱ 15 min Business Lesson 4 of 23

Setting Up the Right Legal Foundation

Running multiple stores without proper legal structure is like building a skyscraper on sand. The structure you choose affects your taxes, liability, and how you manage finances across stores.

Business Entity Options

  • Single LLC — Simplest, one entity for all stores. Good for starting out, but all stores share liability exposure.
  • Multiple LLCs — Each store gets its own LLC. Maximum protection but more administrative overhead.
  • Holding Company Structure — One parent LLC holds multiple subsidiaries. Best of both worlds for serious operators.
  • S-Corp Election — Once profitable ($50K+/year net), can save significant self-employment taxes.
Important: This course provides general educational information, not legal advice. Always consult a business attorney and CPA familiar with e-commerce before making entity decisions.

Separate Bank Accounts per Store

Regardless of entity structure, maintain separate bank accounts and payment processors for each store. This is non-negotiable for clean accounting, tax filing, and understanding per-store profitability.

Sales Tax Considerations

Each new platform and each new state where you establish nexus creates tax obligations. Tools like TaxJar or Avalara automate multi-state sales tax collection and filing — essential for MEO operators.

💰 Key Financial KPIs Per Store

Track: Gross Revenue, Cost of Goods Sold (COGS), Net Profit Margin, Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC), Return Rate, and Average Order Value (AOV) — separately for each store, monthly.
